## KPI & Scaling Guide – Summit Grant Solutions

This guide defines what to track weekly, how to interpret results, and when to scale volume or hire help.

---

## 1. Core KPIs to Track

Use `analytics/kpi-tracking-template.csv` or a Google Sheet with at least these fields:

- **Date**
- **Channel** – Email / LinkedIn / FB / Other
- **Leads Contacted** – number of unique prospects messaged
- **Positive Replies** – “yes”, “tell me more”, “interested”
- **Payments Collected** – number of paying clients
- **Clients This Week** – same as Payments, but can also include non-Amber offers
- **Grant Drafts Delivered** – number of drafts sent to clients
- **Reported Wins** – number of clients who later report winning a grant
- **Revenue (USD)** – total collected revenue for that day/week
- **Notes** – anything unusual (new offer, list source, etc.)

Update this at least **weekly**.

---

## 2. Funnel Benchmarks (Early Stage)

In the first 4–8 weeks, aim for rough ranges:

- **Open rate (email)**: 40–60% once domain is warmed up
- **Reply rate (email)**: 5–15%
- **Positive reply rate**: 2–5%+
- **Lead → Client rate**: 1–3% of contacted leads becoming paying clients

Example:

- Contact 100 leads → 10 replies → 3 positive → 1–2 paying clients

Adjust these benchmarks over time using your real data.

---

## 3. Weekly Review Ritual

Once per week, answer:

1. **How many leads did we contact by channel?**
2. **How many positive replies and clients resulted?**
3. **What was total revenue?**
4. **Which lists or messages performed best?**
5. **Is deliverability healthy (bounces < 5%, spam complaints low)?**

If:

- Reply rate is low → refine targeting and subject lines.
- Positive replies are ok but payments are low → improve your explanation of the $99 offer and remove friction from payment/intake.
- Payments are solid → consider modestly increasing daily volume.

---

## 4. Targets from the Plan

Based on your original notes:

- **Week 1–2**
  - Target: **5–15 clients** total
  - Focus: prove that the offer converts; learn from early calls/emails.
- **Month 2–3**
  - Target: **30–80 clients/month**
  - Focus: reliable lead sources (Apollo/LinkedIn), tighter copy, better list quality.
- **Month 6+**
  - Target: **50–100+ clients/month** + retainer MRR
  - Focus: scale volume safely, delegate repetitive work, add more grant types.

Use these as directional goals, not laws.

---

## 5. When to Hire an Assistant

Consider hiring a **PH assistant** or similar when:

- You consistently hit **10–20+ clients/month**, and
- You feel bottlenecked by:
  - Lead list building and scoring
  - Campaign setup and basic follow-ups
  - Admin tasks around tracking and updating sheets

### 5.1 Tasks to Delegate First

- **Lead work:**
  - Apply the **Grant Virgin scoring SOP** to new leads.
  - Maintain and clean lead lists.
- **Campaign operations:**
  - Upload CSVs to Instantly/Smartlead.
  - Monitor bounces and basic metrics.
- **Admin:**
  - Update client tracking sheet.
  - Move statuses (Intake completed, Draft in progress, Delivered).

Keep **drafting and strategy** on your side until you’re comfortable training others.

---

## 6. When to Add More Grant Types

Once your Amber Grant workflow feels smooth and predictable:

- You know the questions, structure, and what stories work.
- Your drafting time per client is predictable (e.g., 1–2 hours or less).
- You’re seeing consistent demand and a few wins.

Then:

1. Choose **1–2 additional grants** that fit your ICP:
   - Hello Alice (general growth grants)
   - IFundWomen (women-focused private grants)
   - Faire Retail Grant (retail-specific)
2. For each new grant, create:
   - A short **offer description** (who it’s for, what you provide, price).
   - A dedicated **AI prompt template** for drafting.
   - A specific **outreach angle** and email/DM variation.

Add these gradually so you don’t overwhelm your operations.

---

## 7. Simple Scaling Rules of Thumb

- **Rule 1 – Volume follows delivery capacity.**  
  Don’t send more daily outreach than you can comfortably handle as paying clients.

- **Rule 2 – Protect deliverability.**  
  High bounce or spam rates can kill your main asset (your sending domain). Scale slowly and monitor metrics.

- **Rule 3 – Hire for repetitive tasks first.**  
  Offload research, list building, and admin before you delegate storytelling and strategy.

- **Rule 4 – Keep the offer simple.**  
  A clear $99 Amber Grant draft offer that works is more valuable than 10 confusing options.

With this KPI template and guide, you’re ready to track progress toward 10–20 clients, then scale thoughtfully with assistants and additional grant types.
